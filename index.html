Why this works
When two numbers are equidistant from a midpoint m, write them as (m+d) and (m−d).

Their product = (m+d)(m−d) = m² − d²

Squaring the midpoint is easy (pick it to be round). Subtracting d² is easy (d is small). That's it — multiplication becomes subtraction!
